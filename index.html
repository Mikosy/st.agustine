the holy mass

A Sacred ritual for catholics

The Holy Mass is the central act of worship in the Catholic Church, commemorating the Last Supper of Jesus Christ with His disciples. It is a profound celebration where the faithful gather to participate in the Eucharist, receiving the Body and Blood of Christ in the form of bread and wine. Through prayers, hymns, readings from the Scriptures, and the consecration of the Eucharist by the priest.

For Catholics, the Mass is a sacred and transformative encounter with the divine. It serves as both a personal and communal act of faith, allowing participants to reflect on their relationship with God and to seek forgiveness and spiritual nourishment.

Staturory Bodies

The church is made up of three Staturory bodies, the CMO, CWO, CYON. These bodies play significant roles in the life of the Catholic Church, enabling the laity to live out their faith in practical and impactful ways. They provide a structure for men, women, and youth to actively engage in the Church's mission, fostering spiritual growth, community development, and social responsibility.

The Catholic Men Organization (CMO) caters to the spiritual and social needs of men. Its primary mission is to encourage men to take an active role in the life of the Church by living out their faith through prayer, service, and the promotion of Catholic values in their homes and workplaces.

The (CWO)helps women to be active members of the Church, taking on roles that assist in nurturing family life, helping the poor, and promoting moral and ethical values in society.

he Catholic Youth Organization of Nigeria (CYON) is the statutory body responsible for the youth in the Catholic Church. Its mission is to guide the youth in living out their faith in accordance with Catholic teachings, while also preparing them for their roles as future leaders within the Church and society.
